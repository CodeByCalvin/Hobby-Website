Unveiling the Charm of Houseplant Succulents

Houseplant succulents have been the darling of the indoor gardening world for several years now, and for good reason. They bring a unique, exotic touch to any indoor space and offer a robust resilience that makes them easy to care for. In this blog post, we'll explore the top five things you need to know about these adorable, hardy plants.

Diversity and Popularity

Houseplant succulents come in an astonishing variety of shapes, sizes, and colors. Each species has its unique charm, but some have gained more popularity due to their distinctive looks and easy care.

The Jade Plant, with its thick, shiny leaves, and the Aloe Vera, with its healing properties, are two of the most well-known. The Echeveria, known for its rosette shape and vibrant colors, and the Zebra Plant (Haworthia), small and perfect for desktops, are also fan favorites.

Watering routine

Succulents hail from arid regions, so they're adapted to survive with little water. In fact, overwatering is the quickest way to kill a succulent! It's best to water these plants thoroughly but infrequently, letting the soil dry out completely between watering sessions. During the winter, when the plants go dormant, they require even less water.

Soil and potting

Well-draining soil is crucial for healthy succulents. A mix of potting soil with coarse sand, pumice, or perlite is ideal. When it comes to choosing a pot, always go for one with a drainage hole. This allows excess water to escape, preventing root rot. Remember, succulents prefer being a bit snug in their pots, so there's no need to opt for a massive pot for your small succulent.
